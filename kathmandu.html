Kathmandu

Boudhanath Stupa

Boudhanath Stupa, one of the largest stupas in the world and a UNESCO World Heritage Site, is a must-visit landmark in Kathmandu. Located just a few kilometers from the city center, this iconic Buddhist shrine is renowned for its massive white dome and all-seeing eyes of Buddha that gaze in all directions, symbolizing awareness and compassion.

Kathmandu Durbar Square

A UNESCO World Heritage Site, this historical square is home to ancient palaces, temples, and courtyards. Visitors can explore the Hanuman Dhoka Palace, Kumari Ghar (home of the Living Goddess), and intricately carved wooden architecture that reflects Nepal's rich cultural heritage.

Pashupatinath Temple

One of the holiest Hindu temples dedicated to Lord Shiva, Pashupatinath is located along the banks of the Bagmati River. Foreign visitors can witness traditional rituals, cremation ceremonies, and the vibrant cultural atmosphere of this spiritual hub.
